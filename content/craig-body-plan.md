# The Craig Casino Royale Plan
### A 16-week cut for a 43yo with a home gym

---

## The goal, honestly

Daniel Craig in *Casino Royale* sat around **10-12% body fat** with moderate, functional muscle — not bodybuilder mass. You already have muscle under the dad bod. The work is:

1. **Strip 10-15 lbs of fat** over 14-20 weeks (0.75-1 lb/week is the sweet spot — fast enough to see progress, slow enough to keep muscle)
2. **Preserve and slightly build** the muscle you have
3. **Train for density and definition**, not size

Expect visible change by week 4, real change by week 8, and the full result by week 16. At 43, recovery matters — this plan respects that.

---

## Part 1: Nutrition

### The calorie math

Rough starting point — adjust from here based on the scale.

- **Maintenance (sedentary-to-moderate 43yo male):** ~2,400-2,700 kcal
- **Target deficit:** 400-500 kcal/day
- **Daily target:** **~2,000-2,200 kcal**

### Macros (low-carb leaning, high protein)

Protein is non-negotiable on a cut — it preserves muscle while you're eating less.

| Macro | Grams/day | % of calories | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein | 170-190g | ~35% | Muscle preservation, satiety |
| Fat | 80-95g | ~40% | Hormones, satiety, energy on low-carb |
| Carbs | 120-160g | ~25% | Workout fuel, brain function |

If you're ~180 lbs, aim for **~1g of protein per pound of bodyweight**. That's the single most important nutritional lever.

### What to eat

**Proteins (the foundation):**
- Eggs, whole (2-3 per meal is fine)
- Chicken breast, thighs
- Salmon, sardines, tuna, cod
- Lean beef, bison, pork tenderloin
- Greek yogurt (full-fat, unsweetened) — your dairy exception, 2-3x/week
- Cottage cheese if tolerated
- Whey or egg-white protein powder

**Fats:**
- Avocado, olive oil, avocado oil
- Almonds, walnuts, pecans, macadamias
- Fatty fish
- Egg yolks

**Carbs (time them around workouts):**
- Berries, apples, citrus
- Leafy greens (unlimited)
- Cruciferous veg (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts)
- Sweet potato, squash
- Quinoa, lentils, black beans (occasional)
- Oats (pre-workout if you train AM)

**Cut hard:**
- Bread, pasta, rice (except post-workout occasionally)
- Added sugars, sweetened drinks
- Seed oils where possible
- Alcohol — the single biggest fat-loss killer. 2 drinks max per week, ideally zero during the first 8 weeks.

### Daily structure (sample)

**Breakfast** — 3 eggs scrambled in olive oil + avocado + handful of berries. Coffee.

**Lunch** — Large salad: 6oz grilled chicken, mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, 1/4 avocado, olive oil + lemon, pumpkin seeds.

**Pre/post workout** — Greek yogurt with berries and a few walnuts, OR whey shake.

**Dinner** — 6-8oz salmon or steak, roasted broccoli and cauliflower, small sweet potato on training days (skip on rest days).

**If hungry at night** — Hard-boiled eggs, cottage cheese, or just don't eat. Hunger at 9pm is fine; it's not an emergency.

### Non-negotiable rules

1. **Protein at every meal.** No exceptions.
2. **Water: 3+ liters/day.** Most "hunger" at your age is thirst.
3. **Eat 3 meals, skip snacks.** Easier to stay in deficit. If you need a snack, make it protein.
4. **Sleep 7+ hours.** Under-slept = higher cortisol = stubborn belly fat. This matters as much as the diet.
5. **One "off-plan" meal per week.** Not a cheat day — a meal. Enjoy it, move on.

### Track for the first 2 weeks

Use MacroFactor, MyFitnessPal, or Cronometer. Don't guess — people underestimate intake by 30-40%. After 2 weeks of tracking, you'll know your portions by eye and can stop.

## Part 2: Training

### The philosophy

Craig's trainer (Simon Waterson) focused on **compound lifts, circuits, and conditioning** — not isolation bodybuilding. With no barbell, we lean harder on dumbbells, kettlebells, unilateral work, and conditioning. This is actually ideal for the look you want.

### Weekly split (30-40 min sessions)

| Day | Session | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Strength A | Push + Legs |
| **Tue** | Conditioning | Boxing bag + intervals |
| **Wed** | Strength B | Pull + Core |
| **Thu** | Active recovery or 2nd session (see below) | Walk, mobility, or extra conditioning |
| **Fri** | Strength C | Full body / kettlebell complex |
| **Sat** | Conditioning | Longer steady-state + core |
| **Sun** | Rest (full) | Walking fine |

**Double-session days:** If you do 2x/day, pair AM strength + PM conditioning (or vice versa). Don't double up on strength. Back-to-back works for circuit + conditioning combos.

---

### Strength A — Push + Legs (35 min)

Warm-up: 3 min easy treadmill, arm circles, bodyweight squats x10, push-ups x10.

**Circuit 1 — 4 rounds, 60-90s rest between rounds:**
- DB goblet squat — 10 reps
- DB bench press — 8-10 reps
- DB Romanian deadlift — 10 reps

**Circuit 2 — 3 rounds, 45-60s rest:**
- DB shoulder press (seated or standing) — 8-10 reps
- DB Bulgarian split squat — 8 reps/leg
- Push-ups (feet elevated if easy) — AMRAP (as many as possible, stop 2 short of failure)

**Finisher — 5 min:**
- 30s kettlebell swings / 30s rest, repeat

Progression: when you hit the top of the rep range with good form on all sets, increase weight 5-10 lbs next session.

---

### Conditioning — Boxing + Intervals (30-35 min)

This is your fat-loss engine. Craig's physique is as much about conditioning as lifting.

**Option A — Boxing bag (great for upper body + fat loss):**
- 5 min easy treadmill warm-up
- 8 rounds of 2 min bag work / 1 min rest
- Rounds 1-2: jab-cross, light
- Rounds 3-4: add hooks, harder
- Rounds 5-6: 4-punch combos
- Rounds 7-8: all out, last 30s max effort
- 3 min cooldown walk

**Option B — Treadmill intervals:**
- 5 min warm-up
- 10 rounds: 45s hard (8-10 incline or fast pace) / 75s walk recovery
- 5 min cooldown

Alternate these week to week.

---

### Strength B — Pull + Core (35 min)

Warm-up: 3 min treadmill, band pull-aparts or arm swings x20, bodyweight rows on bench x10.

**Circuit 1 — 4 rounds, 60-90s rest:**
- DB single-arm row (bench for support) — 10 reps/side
- DB deadlift (or KB deadlift) — 10 reps
- Inverted row under bench, OR DB pullover — 10 reps

**Circuit 2 — 3 rounds, 45-60s rest:**
- DB hammer curl — 10 reps
- DB rear delt fly — 12 reps
- Farmer's carry (heavy DBs) — 40 seconds

**Core finisher — 3 rounds, no rest within round:**
- Plank — 45s
- Hollow hold — 30s
- Side plank — 30s/side
- Rest 60s between rounds

---

### Strength C — Full Body Kettlebell (30 min)

This is the day that makes you look athletic. Kettlebell complexes build the exact density you want.

**Warm-up:** 3 min easy movement + 10 bodyweight squats, 10 push-ups, 10 glute bridges.

**Complex — do all movements back-to-back without setting the bell down. 5 rounds. Rest 90s between rounds.**

Per side, then switch (or use two KBs if you have them):
- KB clean — 5 reps
- KB front squat — 5 reps
- KB press — 5 reps
- KB row — 5 reps
- KB swing — 10 reps (two-handed)

Start with a moderate weight. If you can't complete 5 clean rounds, go lighter. By week 8, go heavier.

**Finisher — 4 min EMOM (every minute on the minute):**
- 15 KB swings at the top of each minute, rest the remainder

---

### Saturday — Long conditioning + core (40 min)

- 25-30 min steady-state: treadmill incline walk (12-15% incline, 3.0-3.5 mph) OR zone 2 jog
- 10 min core circuit:
  - Dead bug x 10/side
  - Bird dog x 10/side
  - Russian twist (DB) x 20
  - Leg raise x 12
  - Repeat 2-3 rounds

Incline walking is underrated for your goal. It burns fat without trashing recovery like sprints do.

## Part 3: The 16-week structure

**Weeks 1-4 — Adaptation.** Nail the nutrition. Don't worry about weight. Get the movements clean. Energy will dip around week 2, recover by week 3.

**Weeks 5-8 — Acceleration.** Fat loss becomes visible. Increase weights. Add 1 round to circuits if it feels easy. Weigh in weekly.

**Weeks 9-12 — The grind.** This is where most people quit. Progress slows, hunger increases. Hold the line. Consider a "diet break" in week 10 — eat at maintenance for 5-7 days, then resume.

**Weeks 13-16 — Definition.** You'll see the Craig look emerge. Drop calories another 100-150/day if progress stalls. Add 1-2 extra walks per week. Conditioning gets sharper.

---

## Tracking & checkpoints

- **Weigh in:** Same time, 2-3x/week. Track the weekly average, not the daily number.
- **Photos:** Front, side, back. Same lighting, same time of day. Every 2 weeks. You'll see what the scale doesn't.
- **Tape measure:** Waist at navel. This is the single best fat-loss indicator.
- **Lift log:** Track weights and reps. If lifts are going up while weight goes down, you're nailing it.

**Target: lose 1 inch off the waist per month.** That's faster than the scale but more honest about body composition.

---

## When things go wrong

- **Weight stalls 2+ weeks:** Drop calories by 150/day OR add 2 walks/week. Not both.
- **Strength tanks:** You're probably under-sleeping or under-eating protein. Fix those before touching calories.
- **Hunger is unmanageable:** More protein, more fiber, more water. Coffee or green tea between meals helps.
- **Motivation crashes (usually week 6-8):** Normal. Don't quit the plan — reduce intensity for 3-4 days and come back.

> **Eat:** 2,000-2,200 kcal. 180g protein. Mostly meat, fish, eggs, veg, some yogurt and berries. No bread/pasta/rice/booze.
>
> **Train:** 4 strength sessions + 2 conditioning sessions per week. 30-40 min each. Compounds, circuits, kettlebells, boxing bag.
>
> **Sleep:** 7+ hours. Non-negotiable.
>
> **Measure:** Waist, weight, photos. Every 2 weeks.
>
> **Timeline:** 16 weeks. Trust it.
